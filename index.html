About

I am a lecturer at the Lincoln Institute of Agri Foods Technology, where I lead research on autonomous decision-making for robotic systems operating in unstructured field environments.

My doctoral work focused on AI planning and heuristic search; I now apply those techniques alongside modern sensor fusion to problems in the agri-food sector.

Previously: postdoc affiliation, PhD institution.

02

Research

  • Automated Planning

    Domain-independent planning, heuristic search, and plan repair for long-horizon robotic tasks under uncertainty.

  • Sensor Integration

    Multi-modal perception combining RGB-D, LiDAR, and proprioception for robust state estimation in cluttered field environments.

  • Field Robotics

    Deployment of mobile manipulators in agricultural settings — selective harvesting, in-field phenotyping, and autonomous inspection.

  • Human–Robot Teaming

    Mixed-initiative systems where robots and growers share planning responsibility across operational timescales.
